About

Final-year student finishing a dual degree in Mathematics and Computer Science (KREA) alongside a BS in Data Science (IIT Madras). I work at the intersection of computation and pure math, with a preference for problems where you can verify conjectures by brute-forcing them on a cluster.

My current research uses SAT solvers and symbolic regression to push bounds in Ramsey theory. On the applied side, I build computer vision pipelines for esports analytics. I write about all of it on Substack.

Research

Computational Ramsey Theory

Using cube-and-conquer SAT solving (Kissat + march_cu) to compute 2-color Rado numbers for linear equations. Verified the Ahmed-Zaman-Bright conjecture 2.1 and discovered a new pattern: R2(ax + by = az) = a2, verified for a > 30. Applying PySR symbolic regression to search for closed-form expressions across equation families.

SAT solving Ramsey theory symbolic regression combinatorics

Sports-Analytics Computer Vision

Building CV pipelines for competitive VALORANT (VCT NA Challengers). YOLO-based player detection with custom CNNs for scoreboard/map recognition. Active work on map disambiguation (Pearl/Split confusion) and dead-state classification.

YOLO CNN esports computer vision

Domain-Specific Languages for Mathematics

Exploring formal and semi-formal languages for expressing mathematical constructions in ways that bridge human intuition and machine verification. First writeup in progress.

Computational Ramsey Pipeline

End-to-end pipeline for computing Rado numbers. Generates SAT encodings, runs cube-and-conquer with Kissat/march_cu, then feeds results to PySR for symbolic regression. Discovered the R2(ax+by=az) = a2 pattern.
